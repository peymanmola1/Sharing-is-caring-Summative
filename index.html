Puppy Mills

￼

What is it

A puppy mill is a dog breeding facility that focuses on profit with little cost and little care to the health to the dogs and the welfare of the animal not being their main priority.

￼

Breeding Dogs

Female dogs are bred at every opportunity with little to no recovery time between litters.After a few years, when they are physically depleted to the point that they can no longer reproduce they will be killed.Breeding dogs may not even get the chance to see sunlight before they die.

￼

Food & Water

The food and water provided for the puppies is contaminated, crawling with bugs.Puppies can be malnourished and can get infections and diseases.

￼

Living Conditions

In the puppy mills dogs are often caged in a tight space, living in their own faeces and urine.Also since sick dogs are not removed from the caged area, meaning more dogs can become ill.

￼

Veterinary Care

As their is no veterinary care in puppy mills, dogs are often riddle with disease, because of this the puppy will most likely have an medical issue ranging from heart worm to kidney disease.

￼

Injuries

Puppies in mills are often found with bleeding pawsas their feet falling through the wire cages, severe tooth decay, ear infections, dehydration and lesions on their eyes, which will most likley lead to blindness as they dont get treated correctly.

￼

Behaviour

As the puppies are taken away from their mother at such a young age with no chance to bond during the first weeks, which is a crucial stage of their life it may lead to aggression,anxiety or shyness in the adult stage of their life.

￼

After Purchase

On the unfortunate and rare occasion when an innocent person purchases a dog from a pet store, the dog becomes ill and in a worst case scenerio will die, this is because pet shops will sometimes get their stock from puppy mills for cost benefits.

￼

Current Law

The current law means that local authorities are licensed and are allowed to inspect dog breeding establishments within their jurisdiction, which should have gone some way to tackle puppy mills.Their is also a law that means the puppy can not be sold before its 8 weeks old, which is the age that it is ok to leave its mother.

￼

Death

The sad truth is that many puppies do die in the puppy mills this can be from illness and medical problmes or because the puppy mills are too full so they get rid off some dogs or that there are no adoptive homes menaing their are some left over.

￼

Puppies treated Bad

  • At the start of the puppys life they will be thrown into a cage with other puppies and mother dogs.
  • The veterinary care for the dogs is non-existent as they need to be looked after for around 8 weeks.
  • Overcrowding can deform the puppys as at a young age their bones are not the strongest so the bones can be manipulated.
  • For the mother dogs they will be taken away from their puppys and will be bred again quickly with no time to rest and terrible for their health and often killed when they cant reproduce anymore and now by law the mother should not have bred more than six times.
  • The cages that the dogs are kept in are cages with wired flooring which cuts and injures mainly to their paws but also to the body, which can lead to infections.


￼

Puppies treated Good

  • When the puppies are born they should be kept with their mother for 8 weeks before even being consider for sale.
  • The mother and her puppy litter should be kept in a nice clean, comfy space for the important stage of the puppys life.
  • When the buyer goes to collect the puppy the seller should be comfortable with allowing the buyer to see the mother of the puppy.
  • The puppy should be registered albeit under a sort of code name, they will be registered to something like the Kennel Club.
  • During the purchasing of the puppy the seller should advise you on what to feed and what needs to happen regarding vaccinations and when you should get them.
